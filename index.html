Covid-19 in Malaysia

As the Covid-19 cases in Malaysia have been steadily decreasing in the beginning of October, let's take a look at how the situation of Covid-19 in Malaysia have changed since the third lockdown that was imposed on 1 June 2021 by the Malaysian Government.

Cases

Selangor, Kuala Lumpur, and Negeri Sembilan has over 8000 confirmed COVID-19 cases per 100k people in Malaysia which is the
Top 3 highest in Malaysia as of 30 September 2021.

On the other hand, Perlis which located at the most northern part of Malaysia and has a population of approximately 250 thousands contracted the lowest number of COVID-19 cases per 100k people.

How transimible Covid-19 is?

In order to see how transimible COVID-19 is in Malaysia, we can compare the number of cases daily and the number of test taken daily.

There are two types of test in Malaysia which are Polymerase Chain Reaction Test (PCR) and Antigen Rapid Test Kit (RTK).

The average daily cases in August is the highest which is approximately 20 thousands daily. And an average of approximately 150 thousand of tests have been conducted daily in August.

Approximately 13.33% of the test conducted daily would be positive Covid-19 case which could be considered as low.

Vaccination

How's the Vaccination Progress in Malaysia?

62.02% of the population in Malaysia have been fully vacinated, and 10.61% of the population in Malaysia have been partially vaccinated.

A total of 72.63% of Malaysian have at least 1 dose of vaccine as of 30 September 2021. However there are still approximately 27% of the population in Malaysia have not been vaccinated yet

The Malaysian Government has ordered over 76 million doses of vaccines and aims to reach 100% fully vaccinated among the adult's population in Malaysia at the end of October 2021.

Vaccine Brands in Malaysia

The state of Selangor has administred the highest number of vaccines in Malaysia. A total of 4.3 million doses of vaccine have been administred to the people of Selangor.

Among the different vaccine brands, approximately 8.6 million doses of Sinovac have been adminstred to the people as of 30 September 2021.

According to the Malaysian Government, an individual would only be considered fully vacinated 14 days after receiving the second dose of either Pfizer, AstraZeneca or Sinovac vaccines, and 28 days after being jabbed with the single dose of Johnson & Johnson or CanSino vaccines.
